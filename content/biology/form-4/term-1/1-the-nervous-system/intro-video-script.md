[SCENE 3 — WHY IT MATTERS]

"So why should you care about the nervous system?"

(pause)

"Well, apart from the fact that it's literally controlling everything you're doing right now — reading this, breathing, keeping your heart beating — it's also one of the most tested topics in Biology."

[Visual note: show exam paper with "Nervous System" question highlighted]

[SCENE 4 — QUICK TASTE]

PRESENTER:
"Let me give you a quick taste of what we're dealing with."

"Your nervous system has two main parts."

[Visual note: simple diagram — body outline with brain and spinal cord highlighted as "Central Nervous System (CNS)", and lines branching out as "Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)"]

"The Central Nervous System — that's your brain and spinal cord. Think of it as the headquarters."

"And the Peripheral Nervous System — that's all the nerves that branch out to the rest of your body. Think of it as the delivery network."

"Messages travel between these two systems through special cells called neurones. Not neurons with a regular 'o' — neurones. N-E-U-R-O-N-E-S. That's the term we use in Biology."

(pause)

"And there are three types: sensory neurones, motor neurones, and relay neurones. Each one has a different job. We'll break all of that down in the slides."
